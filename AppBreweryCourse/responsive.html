Tips

  • Write your global CSS First
    • This includes items such as picking Colors, Fonts, and Padding
    • Avoid fixed heights and widths, using units such as px
    • You can use properties such as min-width or max-widths for your pages
    • Use adaptable units such as adaptable height or minimum-height instead of a fixed height for a website
  • Use Media Queries to add complexivity to a website
    • Consider using breakpoints at 40 em. This is where screens start to break, and may vary based on a breakpoint.
    • It is also better to have less breakpoints
    • Avoid device specific breakpoints as it is hard to account for every single type of device that exists
  • Viewport heights and widths take into account items such as the browser menu and url input
    • 100vw may give us a horizontal scrollbar because the content is larger than the viewport
    • 100vh can cause issues because an addressbar can take up space. Removing the addressbar can change the appearance of the website.
